System Brief 01 / 06

Editorial is a quiet, typography-first system for spaced-repetition learning. It borrows the discipline of editorial print — generous hierarchy, hairline rules, restrained color — and treats the screen as a page.

One accent. One surface tone. One radius scale. Decisions, not options.

01 / Principles
Editorial behaviour
Four working rules that govern every visual decision in the product.
i.
Type before chrome

Hierarchy is established by weight, scale, and white space — not by boxes, shadows, or accent fills. A heading does the work a card would do elsewhere.

ii.
One semantic accent

Teal #1A8A72 is reserved for state: due cards, active tabs, primary actions, mastery progress. It earns attention because it is rare.

iii.
Hairlines, not shadows

1px borders on warm white separate regions. Shadows are not used. Surfaces are flat and meet at clean edges.

iv.
Numbers are display

Streak counts, queue totals, mastery percentages set in the heaviest grotesk weight at large sizes. Data is the headline.

v.
Cards in a stream, not rows in a table

Lists of decks and queue items are presented as distinct rounded cards on the page canvas with 8px gaps — never flush list-rows. Each card is its own object.

vi.
One elevated action

Create is the one moment shadow appears: a circular FAB raised above the tab bar. Every other surface is flat.
