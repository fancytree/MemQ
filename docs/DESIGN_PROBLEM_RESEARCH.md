# The Design Problem - Research Phase

## Participants & Rounds

**Foundational phase:** 8 in-depth interviews and 24 survey responses exploring learning habits, study pain points, and expectations for AI-powered educational tools. Participants included students preparing for exams, language learners, and professionals seeking skill development.

**Usability testing Round 1:** 6 moderated sessions on the mobile prototype, focusing on core workflows: course creation, PDF import, AI chat interactions, and flashcard learning experience.

**Usability testing Round 2:** 5 additional sessions, including 3 returning participants to observe how familiarity with AI features changed their learning behavior and tool adoption patterns.

**Beta testing phase:** 15 TestFlight users providing feedback on subscription flow, study reminders, and long-term engagement with spaced repetition learning.

## Recruitment & Personas

I recruited participants to match my core personas: **university students** preparing for exams, **language learners** building vocabulary, **working professionals** learning new skills, and **lifelong learners** seeking efficient study methods. Several participants had experience with traditional flashcard apps (Anki, Quizlet) and AI tools (ChatGPT, Claude), which helped identify gaps in existing solutions and opportunities for MemQ's unique value proposition.

Participants were selected to represent diverse learning contexts:
- Students managing multiple courses simultaneously
- Professionals with limited study time seeking efficiency
- Language learners needing vocabulary retention strategies
- Users frustrated with manual flashcard creation processes

## Competitive Analysis

To ground the concept, I conducted a comprehensive review of existing learning and flashcard applications, examining:

**Flashcard & Spaced Repetition Apps:**
- Anki: Powerful but complex interface, steep learning curve
- Quizlet: Social features strong, but limited AI integration
- Memrise: Gamification focus, less flexible for custom content

**AI-Powered Learning Tools:**
- ChatGPT/Claude: Conversational but lacks structured learning framework
- Notion AI: Good for note-taking, weak on active recall methods
- Language learning apps: Strong for vocabulary, limited to specific domains

**Key Gaps Identified:**
- **Fragmented workflows:** Users juggle multiple tools (PDF readers, note apps, flashcard creators, AI chatbots)
- **Manual content creation:** Significant time investment in creating study materials
- **Limited AI integration:** Most flashcard apps don't leverage AI for content generation or personalized assistance
- **Poor PDF integration:** Difficulty extracting structured learning content from documents
- **Generic learning paths:** Lack of personalized, adaptive study experiences

**Opportunities for MemQ:**
- Seamless PDF-to-flashcard conversion using AI
- Integrated AI tutor within learning context
- Unified platform combining content creation, active recall, and progress tracking
- Personalized study recommendations based on learning patterns

## Design Challenges & User Pain Points

**Primary Pain Points Discovered:**

1. **Content Creation Overhead**
   - Users spend more time creating flashcards than studying
   - Manual entry is tedious and error-prone
   - Existing tools require multiple steps to convert PDFs to study materials

2. **Lack of Contextual Help**
   - When stuck on a concept, users leave the app to search for explanations
   - No integrated way to get instant clarification during study sessions
   - Fragmented experience between learning and understanding

3. **Motivation & Consistency**
   - Difficulty maintaining regular study habits
   - No personalized reminders or progress insights
   - Limited visibility into long-term learning outcomes

4. **One-Size-Fits-All Learning**
   - Existing apps don't adapt to individual learning pace
   - Generic spaced repetition schedules don't account for concept difficulty
   - No personalized recommendations based on performance

## Scheduling & Constraints

This was an independent, self-funded project with no formal budget. To respect participants' academic schedules, work commitments, and time zones, I scheduled sessions flexibly—accommodating early morning sessions for professionals, evening sessions for students, and weekend slots for those with busy weekday schedules. Several sessions were conducted remotely via video calls to maximize accessibility and participant comfort.

**Key Constraints:**
- Limited budget for participant incentives
- Diverse time zones (participants from different regions)
- Balancing detailed feedback with session length (aimed for 45-60 minute sessions)
- Technical limitations during early prototype phases (PDF processing accuracy, AI response quality)

**Adaptive Approach:**
- Offered flexible scheduling to accommodate participants' routines
- Provided clear session goals and time estimates upfront
- Created comfortable testing environment (remote options, familiar devices)
- Iterated quickly based on feedback to show progress and maintain engagement

## Research Insights & Design Implications

**Key Insights:**

1. **AI as Learning Accelerator:** Users want AI to reduce friction, not replace learning. They value AI for content generation and explanations, but want to maintain control over their study process.

2. **Context Matters:** Integrated AI chat within the learning flow is more valuable than separate AI tools. Users want to ask questions about specific terms or concepts without leaving their study session.

3. **Trust in Automation:** Initial skepticism about AI-generated content, but high satisfaction once users experienced accurate PDF extraction and relevant question generation.

4. **Progress Visibility:** Users need clear feedback on learning progress to maintain motivation. Visual progress indicators and mastery tracking were consistently rated as high-value features.

**Design Decisions Informed by Research:**

- **PDF Import as Core Feature:** Prioritized seamless PDF-to-flashcard conversion based on strong user demand
- **Integrated AI Chat:** Placed AI assistant within learning context rather than as separate feature
- **Flexible Study Modes:** Allowed users to choose between AI-generated and manually-created content
- **Progress Transparency:** Designed comprehensive progress tracking with visual indicators
- **Habit Formation Support:** Implemented customizable reminders and study streak tracking
